Dear Account Owner,

Tax season is filled with deadlines, computations, and paperwork. But for 529 account owners like you, it's also filled with opportunities.

One of the most obvious opportunities — and easiest to take advantage of — is maximizing the tax benefits your Golden State ScholarShare® College Savings Trust (ScholarShare) 529 account offers. Opening a ScholarShare 529 account gives you a chance to enjoy freedom from California state and federal income taxes on any earnings for your college savings, when withdrawn for qualified education expenses. (The law allowing federal tax-free qualified withdrawals is set to expire on December 31, 2010. Congress may or may not extend the law beyond that date.)

One of the best ways to do it is through the available Automatic Contribution Plan. Tax season is a great time to set it up, since it lets you relax for the rest of the year knowing that you're taking advantage of your 529 account tax benefits. And since you'll be saving for college automatically, you might not even notice your regular investments.

Another way to maximize your tax benefits is by investing any tax refund you might be getting this year into your 529 account. You'll feel great knowing that you're doing all you can for your child — while taking better advantage of the investment opportunities your 529 account offers.

The bottom line is that regular investments could make a difference in the long run. And making an investment is as easy as clicking the button. Why not consider investing right now, while taxes are on your mind?

You deserve a lot of credit for opening a ScholarShare account in the first place. We just wanted to remind you of a few ways you could be taking even better advantage of the tax-advantaged investment opportunities ScholarShare offers.
